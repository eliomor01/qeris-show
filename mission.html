Low-flying coordinated drones

Mission

Low-flying, coordinated drones

Difficult to detect

Small, slow, and operating without emissions, including fiber-optic drones and gliders

Detection by RF and soft-kill jamming is ineffective; radar is limited

Terrain-hugging and hiding

Flying just above the ground allows threats to hide in terrain and background objects

Late detection and line-of-sight systems bring high collateral risk

Evasive maneuvers

Quick and unpredictable changes in speed, altitude, and direction complicate interception

Erratic movements reduce tracking stability and prediction accuracy

Coordinated swarm

Simultaneous attacks adopt different roles, including decoys, relays, strikes, and ingression tactics

Lasers and guns cannot engage multiple directions at once

Interceptors navigate within meters of the ground hunting for threats that hide behind terrain

Multiple drones maximize coverage and handle swarm attacks

  • Low-altitude flight
  • Autonomous
  • Collaborative

Applications

Defense

  • Base and perimeter protection for patrol fences, lines, dead zones, and under canopy
  • Countering low-flying drones
  • Convoy route safety mapping and clearance support

Civilian

  • Search and rescue
  • Vehicle and pedestrian pursuit in dense urban environments
  • Crash and crime-scene first responders
  • Border patrol and area denial for prisons and airports
